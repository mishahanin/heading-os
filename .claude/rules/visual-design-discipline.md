# Visual Design Discipline - Design All Visuals As Made By a Person

> Always-active rule. Applies to every visual artifact 31C ships where 31C identity is implied. Decks (investor, product, keynote, sales), dashboards (bridge daemon, Pulse, briefings, status pages), ODUN.ONE customer-facing surfaces, briefing HTML/PDF, investor materials, the public web. Does NOT apply to internal admin/utility surfaces nobody outside the Tribe sees, code, structured machine output, or third-party content displayed verbatim.
>
> Last Updated: 2026-05-25
> Last Verified: 2026-05-25
> Background and citations: `outputs/research/2026-05-25_design_visual-design-discipline-research.md` (Pass 5 synthesis, 5 research passes, 30 captured screenshots, 20 Odin principles cross-referenced).
> Empirical anchors: `outputs/research/_drafts/exemplars/manifest.json` (Pass 4 visual capture), the embedded screenshot evidence in `reference/visual-design-discipline.md`, Anthropic's own Frontend Design cookbook at platform.claude.com.
> Companion catalog: `reference/visual-design-discipline.md` (exemplar shelf, anti-pattern catalog, audit checklists, vocabulary expansion).

## The frame

Claude is not asked to be a designer. Claude is asked to produce visual artifacts with specific behaviours that, applied consistently, make the result read as designed by a person rather than generated by a default. The behaviours are codified below as five fundamentals plus a vocabulary of AI tells to redirect. The behaviours override the LLM default visual register on every artifact.

The principle behind this rule is that good design and human-reading design are the same thing measured from two ends. AI-default design is the statistical median of the training distribution made visible. It is locally inoffensive at every axis and globally unmistakable when stacked. The cure is parametric: measurable deviation at every axis where defaults exist, with concrete values named and audit gates that count deviations. This is the visual counterpart of `.claude/rules/humanization.md` for prose. The same shape, a different medium.

Rule architecture: parametric leads, identity supplies the specifics. The mechanical fundamentals are constant. The concrete values (brand palette, typeface, spacing tokens) come from 31C's brand authority. The brand authority for 31C is the ODUN.ONE Product Presentation (Master, 12-Apr-2026) at `datastore/products/odun-one/presentations/`. The canonical 31C dashboard implementation is Heading OS v8, scoped in `reference_heading_os_v8_design` and `reference_heading_os_v8_pulse`.

## Step 0 - Test before redesigning (calibration gate, mandatory)

Before applying the rewriting fundamentals below to an existing 31C visual artifact, **test the baseline against the exemplar shelf**. Pull up three references: the artifact under review, the most analogous exemplar from `reference/visual-design-discipline.md`, and one anti-pattern from the same reference. Hold them side by side. Three possible readings:

| Reading | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Artifact reads closer to the exemplar than to the anti-pattern | **Treat as known-good. Preserve byte-for-byte for the surfaces that already work.** Touch only what the explicit task requires. Apply restraint per `.claude/rules/development-standards.md`. A coherent visual register at this score is fragile to incremental edits in the same way detector-tested prose is fragile to punctuation normalisation. |
| Artifact reads halfway between exemplar and anti-pattern | **Diagnose the dominant defect first, then intervene specifically.** See Step 0a below. |
| Artifact reads closer to the anti-pattern than to the exemplar | **Content-first rewrite.** Run the five fundamentals in order, starting from specificity density (real metric names, real numbers, real customer/deal names). Only after that pass consider structural redesign. |

### Step 0a - Diagnose the dominant defect at borderline reading

At borderline readings, layout alone is not the answer. There are at least two distinct defect classes that the rule treats separately, and the right intervention depends on which one is dominant in the specific artifact:

**Defect A: AI-default surface.** Tailwind neutral stack, Inter or Geist alone, rounded-2xl on everything, purple-to-pink hero gradient, three-up feature cards, shadcn sidebar archetype. The artifact reads as scaffolded by v0, Lovable, bolt, Cursor, Gamma, Beautiful.ai, or similar. **When this is dominant, redirect aggressively.** Replace the typeface, replace the palette, break the centered-hero stack, kill the gradient, drop padding by 20-40%, switch from `rounded-2xl` to `rounded-md` or asymmetric radii (4/8/12px on the same view). Re-test.

**Defect B: Content-thin surface.** Layout is reasonable but copy is generic ("Our solution", "Build the future", "AI-powered"), labels are placeholders, numbers are rounded to vanity, charts use synthetic shapes. The artifact reads as a deck or dashboard for a generic company. **When this is dominant, do not redesign.** Replace every label with the actual operator-specific specific. £347,850 not £350,000. The actual customer name. The actual deal stage. The actual referrer. The actual issue ID. Re-test. If the layout still feels wrong, then redesign.

**How to diagnose.** Ask: does this artifact look like it could be from any company in the category, or does it look like 31C specifically? If anonymous, Defect A. If specifically 31C but reading as generic-31C, Defect B. When in doubt, run the smaller intervention (one defect class) and re-test by holding the result against the exemplar shelf.

## The five fundamentals

Ordered by empirical strength. Specificity dominates. Structure is downstream. Apply in order.

### 1. Specificity density (the dominant signal)

**This is the most important fundamental.** Five research passes converged on one finding: the visual register that defeats AI-default detection is not a layout pattern, a palette, or a typeface. It is **real operator data populating real operator labels**. A dashboard that names the actual deal, the actual region, the actual partner is structurally indefeasible in a way that "Total Revenue / New Users / Churn Rate" with synthetic chart shapes is not.

**Every visual surface must contain at least one named, dated, or numbered specific drawn from real operator material.** Real customer names, real deal stages, real numeric values, real metric definitions in operator vocabulary, real timestamps, real referrers, real product strings (`ENG-2703`, `vehicle_state`). Not "Sarah M., Marketing Director." Not "Total Revenue." Not "approximately 50%."

Density benchmark from the Plausible reference dashboard (`plausible.io/plausible.io` is the load-bearing exemplar): every datum on the page is real, every label is the actual metric definition, the URLs in the Top Pages table are the actual top pages of the actual Plausible site. Roughly one named specific per cell of an information-dense view.

**Slide-level translation: the title is the assertion, not the label.** "ODUN.ONE classifies encrypted traffic at line rate without decryption" beats "Our Solution." "Three sovereign-tech POVs across the region chose 31C in Q1 2026" beats "Strong Demand." The slide title carries the argument; the slide body provides the proof.

**Fabricated specifics are worse than missing ones.** If the operator data is unavailable, ask. If it cannot be obtained in this pass, leave the cell empty or use a clearly bracketed placeholder (`[deal name TBD]`) rather than invent.

### 2. Committed stance - one opinionated view

Every artifact takes a position. Refuse to support every option equally. Linear commits to dark. Plausible commits to single-page-no-tabs. PostHog commits to hand-drawn hedgehogs in defiance of every blue-minimal analytics competitor. Mercury commits to commissioned editorial illustration and prints a bi-annual magazine. Notion's whole illustration system is hand-drawn by Roman Muradov.

Tabler's "light/dark parity treated as equal parents, not parent/child" is the signature anti-pattern. So is Salesforce's everything-everywhere. So is the Material 3 design system's tonal-palette extraction that produces a near-uniform primary blue across every implementation that does not customise.

**Concrete moves the rule enforces:**

- **One primary surface mode per artifact.** Dark or light. If both are required, declare one canonical and treat the other as derived, not co-equal. Heading OS v8 is the canonical 31C dashboard pattern for this commitment.
- **One signature interaction or visual move per surface.** Linear's collapsible sidebar. Plausible's single-page layout. The bridge dashboard's sync-pill. The 31C orange corner block (per `feedback_design_standards`). Refuse to multiply signatures.
- **Refused symmetry.** When the artifact admits a both-sides framing, take one side anyway and name it. Misha's voice rule already enforces this for prose; the visual rule extends it.

### 3. Hierarchy by intent (not uniform grid)

AI dashboards fail at hierarchy because RLHF rewards "balanced." Every metric card identical width, every chart identical height, every padding token the same. Real operator dashboards have **heterogeneous card weight**. The money metric occupies 60% or more of viewport above-fold. Everything else is supporting context. Datadog's density is legitimate because SRE specialists are trained on the chunks; Salesforce's density is illegitimate because every tile gets equal weight regardless of operator priority.

**Slide-level: the money slide.** One chart or number anchors the argument, gets full bleed, no decoration. Every other slide is supporting context. Stripe's keynote register does this aggressively: three data points centered on a slide, the speaker carries the rest, one Patrick Collison opener with no title at all because the audience already knows what they came to hear.

**Parametric rule (auditable):**

- Heading-to-body type-size ratio ≥3.5x. If the headline is 56px, body cannot exceed 16px.
- Exactly one element per slide above 60% canvas height. Two equal-weight focal elements = no focal element.
- Bento tiles with equal sizes are a card layout in disguise. Mandate at least one tile at 2x the area of the smallest tile in any bento view.
- Above-fold cap: at most 4 discrete elements (Pass 4 audit confirmed Linear, Vercel, Stripe, Resend, Plausible all stay under 4).

### 4. Restricted palette, signature accent

**One brand base. One strong accent. Three to five greys tinted toward the brand hue.** No two saturated colors at hero density. No gradient hero text. No purple-to-pink hero gradient (the single most-cited tell across every source - Anthropic's own cookbook bans it as the "AI slop signature").

**Parametric rule:**

- Delta-E ≥12 between non-adjacent elements (Grok's measurable contrast rule from the council pass).
- One saturated accent per surface. If the brand has two (31C orange + ODUN blue per `feedback_design_standards`), one leads per surface; the other is supporting.
- Gradient backgrounds are permitted only when the gradient is a commissioned brand asset, not a CSS template. Stripe's purple-magenta-coral ribbon (commissioned) is permitted in spirit. Vercel's brand-orange radial (commissioned) is permitted in spirit. `bg-gradient-to-r from-purple-500 to-pink-500` (Tailwind default) is forbidden.
- Pastel violet-pink-lavender at hero density is forbidden. Material 3's `~#E8DDF4`, Gamma's `~#D0E4F5`, Tabler's `~#F5EEF8` are the captured tells. If a pastel in this band sits at hero density, the artifact fails.

### 5. Typography chosen with intent

Anthropic's frontend cookbook explicitly bans Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Lato, and default system fonts. The 31C workspace already commits to GT Standard for documents (per `feedback_corporate_fonts_always` and `reference_brand_assets`). The new rule extends commitment to interface design.

**Permitted as primary:**

- **GT Standard family** for all 31C corporate documents (proposals, letters, partnership docs, official docs, xPagers, briefing HTML/PDF). This is locked per the corporate-docs guardrail and `reference/corporate-style-guide.md`.
- **Geist family** (Geist Sans, Geist Mono, Geist Pixel) for public-web surfaces where Vercel's commissioned register resonates. Geist is in 2026 the new statistical median for developer-aimed sites; use with awareness, prefer a custom variant where 31C overlap with Vercel identity becomes a tell.
- **IBM Plex family** (Sans, Mono, Serif) as the open-source fallback when GT Standard is unavailable in the target system.
- **Custom commissions** for high-stakes surfaces where the typographic signature is part of the brand argument.

**Forbidden as primary:**

- Inter (Pass 1 cited: "growing ubiquity in the SaaS industry means using it may make your product look like every other SaaS product").
- Roboto, Open Sans, Lato, Poppins, Montserrat (the budget-tier defaults).
- Default system fonts when the artifact has 31C identity.

**Permitted patterns:**

- Weight extremes per Anthropic: 100/200 vs 800/900, not 400 vs 600.
- Size jumps of 3x or more between hierarchy levels, not 1.5x.
- Negative tracking on display (Linear's `-0.22px` at 56px is the reference).
- Maximum two typefaces per artifact, ideally one. Mixing sans and serif on the same slide requires a defensible reason.

## The vocabulary of AI tells

Catalog of patterns the rule names for redirection. Full per-tell expansion lives in `reference/visual-design-discipline.md`. The summary below is the audit checklist.

| Category | Tells to forbid or redirect |
|---|---|
| Color | Purple-to-pink hero gradient, indigo-violet primary accents (Tailwind defaults), legacy ChatGPT emerald `#10A37F` as primary, pastel rainbow gradient blobs, the Tailwind neutral stack (`slate-50` + `zinc-900`) without modification |
| Typography | Inter / Roboto / Open Sans / Lato / Poppins / Montserrat as primary, Space Grotesk as the "I tried" upgrade, JetBrains Mono code overlays on non-devtool products, mid-weight monoculture (font-medium headings + font-normal body) |
| Layout | Centered-hero stack (eyebrow + 64pt headline + subhead + two CTAs), three-up icon-and-description feature cards in `rounded-2xl`, the shadcn dashboard archetype (sidebar-left, table-center, button-group-top-right), equal-size bento tiles, "Trusted by" logo strip immediately under the hero, FAQ accordion + three-column footer, big empty hero with no message |
| Components | The shadcn card composition (Avatar + Title + Description + optional Action), Lucide outline icons at 24px / 1.5px stroke / rounded caps as default, Heroicons outline as the Tailwind-house alternative, pricing toggle + three-tier cards with "Recommended" outline |
| Motion | Framer Motion `type: "spring"` with default `bounce`, stagger-reveal on page load, hover-scale-1.02 on cards, universal 200-300ms ease-out transitions |
| Assets | AI-generated decorative illustrations tonally matched to the palette (the Gamma giveaway), the isometric 3D illustration return, default Unsplash stock with shallow DOF and cool grading |
| Copy register | "Build the future" / "AI-powered" / "Reimagine X" taglines, "Ship X. Build Y." imperative pairs, generic CTAs ("Get started", "Learn more"), em-dash overuse in feature copy, Title Case For Every Heading |
| Dashboard composite | shadcn sidebar + 4-stat-card row with Lucide icons and "vs last month" deltas + Recharts area chart card + data table with avatar+name+badge+amount rows + `+ New` button in top-right primary accent (v0 "Dashboard 01" template) |
| Presentation composite | Vertical card layout (Gamma's signature), purple-to-blue background, Inter alone on every slide, three-card feature row, generic titles ("Our solution", "Why us"), hallucinated testimonials ("Sarah M., Marketing Director"), Title Case headings, brand voice not carrying through visually |
| Enterprise refusal | Site behind Akamai bot detection (Salesforce, SAP, ServiceNow refuse headless visitors). Sovereign-tech that mimics this defensive funnel posture inherits the defensive register |

The single structural pattern that is always banned: **Title Case For All Headings**. Use sentence case. Title Case is print-magazine convention that RLHF picked up from training data.

## The asset rule

Stock people-photography is banned. This includes generic offices, hands at keyboards, lightbulbs, gears, three-people-pointing-at-a-laptop, diverse teams in branded T-shirts, founders-in-suits portraiture, customer-headshots-on-coloured-circles.

**Custom-generated illustration is permitted only under one of two constraints:**

- **2-bit monochrome constraint.** Two values, one accent. Line art at line weight matched to the artifact's body type. Examples in the exemplar shelf: Stripe Press editorial illustrations, Notion's Roman Muradov ink work, basic Tribe maritime metaphor illustrations.
- **Commissioned-style register.** The illustration must read as commissioned editorial (Mercury), commissioned animation (Active Theory), or commissioned mascot (PostHog's hedgehog). AI-generated illustrations that tonally match the slide palette are forbidden because they read as the Gamma signature.

Real product screenshots are always permitted. Real Tribe photography is always permitted. Real customer-installation photography is always permitted when the customer has cleared distribution.

## The carve-out: when defaults are permitted

This rule is register-conditional. Internal admin and utility surfaces are exempt because the AI-default register on those surfaces is not register-conditional brand surface. Tabler, AdminLTE, or default Material 3 on an internal-only ops dashboard nobody sees outside the Tribe carries no identity penalty.

**In scope (full rule applies):**

- Investor materials, board materials, fundraising decks, data room artifacts.
- Customer-facing 31C product UI (ODUN.ONE analyst dashboard, ODIN AI surfaces, TrustONE customer console).
- The bridge daemon dashboard, Pulse, Day, Inbox, Conversations pages (canonical implementation: Heading OS v8).
- Marketing site, product pages, status pages.
- Briefing HTML and PDF (intel-briefing-newsletter, market-brief, ceo-intel, dashboard, weekly-review outputs).
- Decks: keynote-deck, pptx-generator outputs, investor-pitch outputs, proposal renders.
- Corporate documents per the five locked doctypes (corporate-letter, proposal, partnership-doc, official-doc, xpager).

**Out of scope (rule does not apply):**

- Internal ops scripts, daemon logs, machine output.
- Code, JSON, structured machine data viz consumed by Claude or by automation.
- Throwaway HTML reports for personal CEO use (`outputs/operations/`, `outputs/clipboard/`).
- Third-party content displayed verbatim (citations, quoted material, brand assets from counterparties shown for reference).

## Validation requirement

Every visual deliverable presented to Misha must include the standard confirmation line:

> Visual register: [register name]. AI tells found: clean / N findings (and a one-line summary of redirects applied if any). Specificity density: X named specifics per artifact.

The mechanical tells are audited by `scripts/visual-discipline-check.py` (shipped 2026-06-26): it scans HTML/SVG/PPTX for forbidden fonts, the purple->pink gradient, oversized Tailwind radii (`rounded-2xl`/`3xl`), Lucide/Heroicons defaults, and the banned hero colors (ChatGPT emerald, captured pastels) as errors, plus advisory layout/copy heuristics (neutral-stack pairing, indigo-violet primary, three-up cards, centered hero, Title Case headings). Run `python scripts/visual-discipline-check.py <file-or-dir>` (`--strict` to fail on warnings, `--json` for programmatic use; out-of-scope internal dirs are skipped unless `--include-internal`). The first three fundamentals - specificity density, committed stance, hierarchy by intent - are not mechanically checkable and remain human-judged against the exemplar shelf. The CEO can request the audit at any time during a session.

For artifacts produced by skills (`/design`, `/pptx-generator`, `/keynote-deck`, `/dashboard`, `/marp`, `/xpager`, `/intel-briefing-newsletter`, `/market-brief`, `/proposal`, `/corporate-letter`, `/partnership-doc`, `/official-doc`, `/investor-pitch`, `/data-room`, `/docparse`), the producing skill is responsible for running the audit before declaring the artifact done. Skills must invoke this rule's checklist as part of their phased execution.

## Failure modes to watch

- **Over-scrubbing without replacing (the vacuum trap).** Banned-pattern removal that leaves nothing in its place produces empty, lifeless artifacts. Removal must be paired with replacement: kill the gradient, name the brand voltage that replaces it.
- **Mechanical 1-spread-1-tight parametric rhythm.** Mechanical compliance with the deviation count (every artifact has exactly 4 deviations on a "human offset" layer) is itself a pattern an audit will eventually recognize. The rule mandates *deliberate variance*, not a metronome of variance.
- **Forced specifics that are fabricated.** "ODUN.ONE saved Customer X 17 days of incident response time" is humanising only if it is also true. Inventing specifics is worse than generalising. When the specifics are unknown, ask Misha or leave a bracketed placeholder.
- **Identity-level mimicry without substrate.** "Use 31C brand voice" applied without consulting the Product Deck produces caricature, not voice.
- **Ignoring the rule on "internal" output that is in fact external.** An "internal" dashboard that becomes a screenshot in a press piece was not internal. When in doubt about scope, apply the rule.
- **Treating Heading OS v8 as a template to copy verbatim.** v8 is the canonical implementation of the principles, not a frame to extract pixel-by-pixel for every new dashboard. The principles are portable; specific layouts are scoped to specific surfaces.

## When the rule cannot apply

Four exceptions and only four:

1. **Internal admin and utility surfaces** per the carve-out above.
2. **Code, configuration, structured data viz** consumed by Claude, by daemons, or by automation. Not user-facing visual artifacts.
3. **Third-party content displayed verbatim.** Citations, quoted reports, counterparty brand assets shown for reference.
4. **CEO-supplied draft Claude is rendering.** When Claude is the producer, the rule applies. When Claude is rendering a CEO-supplied PSD, sketch, or hand-marked annotation, follow the CEO's choices and surface concerns only on confirmation.

## Change control

Updates to this rule require Misha's explicit approval. The vocabulary of AI tells is expected to drift on a 6 to 9 month cadence as the dominant generators update their defaults and the design discourse identifies new tells. The Last Verified date triggers a re-audit when older than 9 months.
