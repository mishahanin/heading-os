# 31 Concept - Tone of Voice

> The captain's voice on the bridge. Direct, warm, authoritative, story-driven.
> For the complete voice reference, see: `reference/misha-voice.md`

---

## Voice Character

31C's voice is Misha Hanin's voice - a founder with 30+ years in cybersecurity, a sailor, a technical diver. He speaks with earned confidence. Statements of fact, not hedged opinions. Maritime metaphors arise naturally because they're how he thinks. The tone is a captain addressing experienced professionals, not a marketer addressing prospects.

---

## Core Attributes

### 1. Authentic Over Corporate

Real language beats polished language. "We built this from zero lines of code" beats "We developed a proprietary solution." Say it plainly. If it sounds like it came from a press release, rewrite it.

### 2. Direct and Action-Oriented

Lead with the point. The first sentence carries the weight. Don't build up to the insight - start with it, then support it. Cut the second half of every sentence until it hurts.

### 3. Warm but Authoritative

The captain is approachable but unquestionably in command. Warmth comes from honesty and shared experience, not from exclamation marks or corporate cheerleading.

### 4. Story-Driven When It Matters

Open with a counterintuitive observation, a lesson from the water, or a challenge to conventional thinking. Build tension. Land the insight. The narrative is the argument.

## Five Core Principles (As States, Not Rules)

When referencing 31C principles in slides, frame them as operational states the Tribe maintains:

1. **Proof of Value over PoC** - "We exist in a condition where every engagement proves real-world value before a commercial conversation begins."
2. **Partnership for Life** - "Every interaction deepens trust rather than extracts value."
3. **Operate with Integrity** - "Integrity is the default operating mode, especially when nobody would notice."
4. **Deliver Under Pressure** - "Pressure reveals performance that was already there."
5. **Data Sovereignty Always** - "Sovereignty isn't a feature. It's the condition our entire company exists within."

## Writing Guidelines for Slides

### Do
- Use short, punchy sentences (one idea per bullet)
- Lead with the insight, not the context
- Use specific numbers ("[N]+ Tribe members across [M] countries" not "a large global team")
- Frame achievements as natural outputs of operational states
- Use maritime metaphors when they illuminate
- Use hyphens (-), never em-dashes

### Don't
- Say "excited to announce" or "thrilled to share"
- Use "synergies," "ecosystem," "disruptive," or any corporate filler
- Reference competitors by name
- Use military language (maritime only)
- Put emojis in slide content
- Use more than 6 lines of text per slide
- Hedge with qualifiers ("We believe we might be...")

## Formatting Rules

- **Hyphens only** - Use (-), never em-dash or en-dash. Zero exceptions.
- **the legacy incumbent reference** - "CCAA protection" or "bankruptcy," never "Chapter 11"
- **Scale reference** - "[N]+ Tribe members" (avoid "across [M] countries" unless specifically relevant)
- **ODUN.ONE** - Always caps with period: ODUN.ONE
- **31C** - Abbreviation for 31 Concept
- **DPI+** - Always with plus sign
