# Test Prompts: deal-strategy

## Should Trigger (positive)
Realistic, messy prompts that SHOULD activate this skill.

- "how do we win the ExampleTelco deal"
- "competitive positioning for the ExampleTelco opportunity -- they're comparing us to the legacy incumbent"
- "analyze this deal: Meridian Mobile wants a sovereign DPI solution for their regional operation"
- "deal strategy for Nimbus Telecom, they have budget but procurement is slow"
- "what's our play against the incumbent in this RFP?"
- "I need a strategy for the Atlas Telecom pitch next week -- $2M deal, they care about sovereignty"
- "how should we price this for a Tier 1 African telco, 50M subscribers"
- "the Nimbus Telecom deal is stuck -- they want a PoC but we need a PoV approach, help me figure this out"
- "evaluate this opportunity: government tender in Saudi for national DPI platform"
- "what objections will we face from ExampleTelco and how do we handle them"

## Should NOT Trigger (negative)
Prompts that are similar but should NOT activate this skill.

- "dig into ExampleTelco's background" (OSINT research, not deal strategy)
- "prepare me for the call with ExampleTelco" (meeting-prep, not strategic analysis)
- "write a proposal for Nimbus Telecom" (proposal skill)
- "draft the RFP response for Saudi" (rfp-response skill)
- "update the ExampleTelco contact in CRM" (CRM operation)
