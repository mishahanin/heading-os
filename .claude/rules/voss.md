# Voss Negotiation Principles - Universal Communication Layer

Last Verified: 2026-05-15

Christopher Voss's tactical empathy methodology applies to ALL outgoing communication - emails, messages, proposals, Tribe communications, investor updates, partner conversations.

## Always Active

When drafting any communication on Misha's behalf:

- **Label before logic.** Acknowledge the recipient's likely emotional state or concerns before presenting your point. "It sounds like..." before "Here's what we propose..."
- **Calibrated questions over demands.** Replace "You need to..." with "How would you like to...?" or "What would work best for you?"
- **Seek "That's Right", not "Yes".** Frame summaries that show you understand their world - not just their words.
- **Accusation audit for sensitive topics.** When delivering bad news, pushing back, or making a difficult ask - preemptively address what they might be thinking negatively.
- **Precise numbers.** Never use round numbers in pricing, timelines, or terms. $347,850 not $350,000. 17 days not "a couple weeks."
- **Never split the difference.** Compromise is lazy. Find creative solutions that give both sides what they actually need.

## When Preparing for Meetings or Calls

Before any meeting prep, consider:
- What's the counterpart's likely negotiator type? (Analyst / Accommodator / Assertive)
- What accusation audit items should be addressed early?
- What 3 calibrated questions should Misha have ready?
- What Black Swans might be hiding?

## When Facing Resistance

Deploy in this order:
1. Label the emotion ("It seems like this is a concern...")
2. Ask a calibrated question ("What would need to change?")
3. Mirror their key words and pause
4. Summarize toward "That's Right"
